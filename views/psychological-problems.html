When to Seek Professional Help

Immediate Concerns

  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Severe mood swings or psychotic symptoms
  • Inability to function in daily life
  • Substance abuse affecting health
  • Risk of harm to others

Early Intervention Signs

  • Persistent changes in mood or behavior
  • Difficulty maintaining relationships
  • Declining work or academic performance
  • Social withdrawal and isolation
  • Unexplained physical symptoms

Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Early intervention can prevent conditions from worsening and improve your quality of life significantly.
